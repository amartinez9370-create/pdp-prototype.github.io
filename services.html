Dental Services

We offer a full range of preventive, restorative, and cosmetic services designed to protect your oral health and enhance your smile. With modern techniques, gentle care, and personalized treatment plans, we make sure every visit is a comfortable one.



Oral Check-up


A complete check-up of your mouth, teeth, and gums. The dentist examines for cavities, gum disease, and other oral health issues. May include X-rays if needed, to detect problems early.


Cleaning (Phrophylaxis)


Professional removal of plaque, tartar, and stains that can’t be removed by brushing alone. Helps prevent gum disease and tooth decay while keeping your smile fresh.


Dental Fillings


Used to repair cavities or small cracks in teeth. The decayed portion is removed and replaced with composite (tooth-colored) material to restore shape and function.


Extraction


Safe and gentle removal of a tooth that’s too damaged, decayed, or impacted to be saved. Usually done under local anesthesia to ensure comfort.


Whitening


A safe, professional bleaching process that removes surface stains and lightens teeth for a noticeably brighter smile.


Dental Veneers


Thin, custom-made porcelain or composite shells placed on the front surface of teeth to improve color, shape, and alignment. Perfect for chipped, discolored, or uneven teeth.


Dental Crowns


Dental Crowns are protective “caps” placed over weak or damaged teeth to restore their shape, strength, and appearance. Often made of porcelain, ceramic, or metal.


Dental Bridges


Dental Bridge is a fixed replacement for one or more missing teeth. Bridges use neighboring teeth or implants for support and restore normal biting and chewing.


Dentures (Full or Partial)


Removable appliances designed to replace multiple missing teeth. Full dentures replace all teeth in an arch; partial dentures replace a few missing ones.


Dental Implants


A permanent solution for missing teeth. A titanium post is surgically placed into the jawbone and topped with a crown, creating a natural-looking and long-lasting tooth replacement.


Braces


Braces are metal or ceramic brackets attached to teeth and connected by wires to gradually move teeth into proper alignment.


While Retainers are custom-made devices worn after braces or aligners to keep teeth in their new, corrected positions.
