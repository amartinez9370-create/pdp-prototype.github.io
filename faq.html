Frequently Asked Questions

We believe that informed patients make the best decisions for their oral health. Explore our most frequently asked questions below by category.

Oral Check-up

It’s generally recommended to visit the dentist every 6 months for a routine check-up and cleaning. However, your dentist may suggest more frequent visits based on your oral health needs.

Not necessarily. Dental X-rays are taken only when needed to check areas not visible during a regular exam. Your dentist will assess your risk factors and history first.

Cleaning (Prophylaxis)

Professional cleaning is usually painless, though you might feel slight sensitivity if your gums are inflamed. Regular cleanings help prevent discomfort in the future.

A routine cleaning typically takes 30 to 45 minutes, depending on the amount of plaque or tartar buildup.

Fillings & Extractions

Composite fillings can last 5–10 years or more with good oral hygiene and regular dental visits.

No. Local anesthesia is used during extractions to make the procedure comfortable. You may feel pressure but not pain.

Whitening & Cosmetic Dentistry

Yes, professional whitening is safe when done under a dentist’s supervision. It uses controlled whitening agents that won’t damage enamel.

Porcelain veneers can last 10–15 years or more with proper care, such as avoiding hard foods and maintaining regular dental check-ups.

Crowns, Bridges & Implants

A crown covers a single damaged tooth, while a bridge replaces one or more missing teeth using adjacent teeth for support.

Yes, dental implants are designed to be a permanent tooth replacement solution, provided they’re properly cared for with good oral hygiene.

Orthodontics (Braces & Retainers)

Braces are effective at any age, but treatment is often easiest during early adolescence when the jaw is still growing.

Most patients wear retainers full-time for a few months after braces, then nightly indefinitely to maintain alignment.
